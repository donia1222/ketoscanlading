FAQ

How does the app work?

Keto Scanner allows you to analyze products to know if they are compatible with the ketogenic diet.

Steps to use the app:

  1. Press the "Scan Product" button
  2. Point the camera at the product barcode
  3. The app automatically gets nutritional information
  4. You receive a 1-5 star rating based on how keto-friendly the product is

Additional features:

  • History: Save your previous scans
  • Favorites: Mark products you like
  • Community: See what products other users scan
  • Trending: Discover the most popular products

What are net carbs?

Net carbs are the carbohydrates that your body actually absorbs and affect your blood sugar levels.

Formula:

Net Carbs = Total Carbohydrates - Fiber

Why does fiber matter?

Fiber is a type of carbohydrate that your body cannot digest, so it doesn't affect ketosis.

Example:

  • Product with 15g carbs and 8g fiber
  • Net carbs = 15 - 8 = 7g

In the keto diet:

Most people on keto try to consume less than 20-50g of net carbs per day to stay in ketosis.

How is the rating calculated?

The 1-5 star rating is mainly based on net carbohydrates per 100g of the product.

Excellent - Up to 2g net carbs - Ideal for keto
Very good - 2-5g net carbs - Good option
Moderate - 5-10g net carbs - Consume in moderation
Limited - 10-20g net carbs - Use occasionally
Not recommended - >20g net carbs - Avoid

Additional factors:

  • High sugar (>5g) penalizes rating
  • High fat and moderate protein are positive

Where does the data come from?

All nutritional information comes from Open Food Facts, a collaborative and free database.

About Open Food Facts:

  • Open and free database
  • More than 3 million products
  • Maintained by volunteers worldwide
  • Community-verified data

How does it work?

When you scan a product, the app queries the Open Food Facts API and gets:

  • Product name
  • Brand
  • Nutritional information
  • Product image

Contribute:

If a product is not in the database, you can add it at: world.openfoodfacts.org

Note: Nutritional data may vary by region and product version.

What is the keto diet?

The ketogenic (keto) diet is a low-carb, high-fat eating plan that puts your body into a metabolic state called ketosis.

Typical keto macros:

  • 70-75% fats
  • 20-25% proteins
  • 5-10% carbohydrates

What is ketosis?

It's a metabolic state where your body uses fat as its main fuel instead of carbohydrates.

Keto-friendly foods:

Meats and fish Eggs Cheeses and full-fat dairy Avocados Nuts and seeds Leafy green vegetables Healthy oils

Foods to avoid:

Sugars and sweets Grains and cereals High-sugar fruits Legumes Tubers

Important: Consult a health professional before starting any diet.
